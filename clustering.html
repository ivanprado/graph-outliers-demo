Clustering coefficient

Clustering coefficient is a measure between [0 and 1] of how close a graph is to form a clique. Graph A is a clique, graph B is a star.

The clustering coefficient of a large graph is not very useful, however the individual clustering coefficient of each egonet may be key to detect suspicious or anomalous behavior. Highly clustered egonets might show strange spammer-like associations, suspicious trading, etc. On the other side, and depending on the context, loosely clustered egonets might be strange as well (for instance, robot-like behavior such as port scanning).

Instead of thresholding on clustering coefficients, a better technique consists in plotting a graph where X is the number of nodes in the egonet and Y the number of connections in the egonet. By running a regression on the resultant dataset, points that deviate significantly from the regression can be detected as outliers.
