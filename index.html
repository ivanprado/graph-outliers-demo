But we were looking for outliers, weren't we?

What about the degree distribution?

The degree distribution shows nothing interesting. Some nodes will have far more connections than the rest, and there is nothing strange in that. Or can't Lady Gaga have 38M followers on Twitter? A long tail of nodes will have very few connections as well, and that's also pretty normal.

At the end of the day, the degree distribution of a social network will be just some kind of zipfian function.

What outliers are there then?

However, when comparing the degree of a node with the size of its egonet, relevant outliers may arise. It is intuitive to think that the size of a node's egonet will increase with its degree. In fact, there is a power law between both factors.

By running a regression (using min. squares) on the egonet size based on the degree, we obtain an "expected egonet size" line. Using this line, we see now two types of interesting outliers:

  • Nodes who have far more connections in its egonet than expected: These nodes may be artifically connected (e.g. spam, fake accounts that are interlinked for gaining influence in a network).
  • Nodes who have far less connections in its egonet than expected: These may be nodes who randomly and massively connect to other nodes, whose activity in a social network is not pure or legitimate. However, some profiles such as professional recruiters may fall into this category as well.

Did you know?

The idea of performing a regression on the Degree versus the Egonet size is presented in this paper under the name "Egonet Density Power Law", and has been tested with several datasets.

The above graphs are directly related to the clustering coefficient of the egonet.

In this paper, ... conducted an study and showed that the number of triangles in an egonet (which is another way of measuring how clustered an egonet is) proved a good measure for spam profile detection, and they validate their hypothesis with Twitter data, successfully discriminating between just popular profiles and artifically interlinked spam accounts.
